Application stage

This project is at the pre-application stage.

Pre-application
Completed

The pre-application process for Crown development applications is handled by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG). Together the applicant and MHCLG will identify any issues there may be with reference to the development plan, the National Planning Policy Framework, planning practice guidance documents and issues raised by pre-application community consultation. These will be addressed in the application submission.

Accepted
Completed

This is the stage added once the application has been deemed to be of national importance by SoS. The is when case is published but not yet open for consultation.

The Planning Inspectorate (PINS) will assess the application to check that it is complete (i.e., that the required documentation has been submitted and the correct fee paid). The Secretary of State will assess whether the application is considered to be of national importance. Once these are confirmed, the application is accepted for consideration and is published.

Consultation
Completed

The relevant Local Planning Authority (LPA) notifies anyone who has been consulted and publicises the fact that a Crown Development application has been made and that PINS is accepting written representations in response to the application from statutory consultees and other interested parties. The time limit for submitting written representations is no less than 21 days from the date the application is accepted. Any representations submitted after this date may not be taken into account.

Procedure decision
Completed

At the end of the consultation period, the Planning Inspectorate will determine whether the application should proceed by written representations, hearing or inquiry. The decision will be communicated to the applicant, relevant LPA and anyone who submitted comments during the representations period.

Written representations
Completed

The chosen procedure for this application is by written representation. Where an application is determined through the written representations procedure, the application will be determined by the appointed person on the basis of written submissions usually following a site inspection.

Inquiry
Completed

The chosen procedure for this application is by inquiry. The inquiry is open to the public and provides for the investigation into, and formal testing of, evidence, usually through cross examination of expert witnesses and other witnesses. Parties may be formally represented by advocates.

The applicant, relevant LPA and any statutory party will be able to submit a statement of case. This must be submitted within 5 weeks of the date they are notified of the inquiry.

The Inspector or, as the case may be, panel of Inspectors, under rule 13(3) of the 2025 Hearings and Inquiries rules, may in writing require any person that has made a request to appear at the inquiry to also submit a statement of case. This must be submitted within 4 weeks of our writing to them.

Proofs of evidence must be submitted no later than 4 weeks before the inquiry date. If they exceed 1500 words, they should be submitted with a summary.

A case management conference (CMC) will likely be held in the run up to the inquiry. The Planning Inspectorate will notify anyone entitled to attend the inquiry of the details and circulate an agenda in advance.
