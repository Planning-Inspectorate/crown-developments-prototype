Application stage

This application is at the accepted stage.

Pre-application
Completed

The pre-application process for Crown Development applications is handled by the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG).

Together, the applicant and MHCLG identify any issues there may be with the:

  • development plan
  • National Planning Policy Framework
  • planning practice guidance documents
  • issues raised by pre-application community consultation

These will be addressed in the application submission.

Accepted
Not started

The Planning Inspectorate (PINS) checks that the application is complete. For example, that the right documents and fees have been added.

The Secretary of State assesses whether the application is of national importance. Once this is confirmed, the application is accepted for consideration and is published.

Consultation
Not started

The relevant Local Planning Authority (LPA) publicises the fact that a Crown Development application has been made. The LPA notifies anyone who has been consulted.

The Planning Inspectorate (PINS) accepts written representations from statutory consultees and other interested parties.

The time limit for submitting written representations is no less than 21 days from the date the application is accepted. Any representations submitted after this date may not be taken into account.

Procedure decision
Not started

The procedure is the method of decision process the Planning Inspectorate will use to make a decision about the proposed development.
The Planning Inspectorate will keep the procedure choice, including combined procedures, under review. Subject to any notification and procedural requirement, the procedure may be changed at any point before the decision is issued.

The planning inspector will determine how the application should proceed. The possible procedures are:

  • written representations
  • hearing
  • inquiry

The decision will be communicated to the applicant, relevant Local Planning Authority (LPA) and anyone who submitted comments during the representations period.

Reporting and decision
Not started

The inspector uses the evidence to write a decision report about the outcome of the application.

The Planning Inspectorate (PINS) issues a formal decision notice including a statement with the reasons for the decision.

If the application is approved, the decision will also list any necessary conditions.

Post-decision
Not started

Where permission has been granted on a Crown Development application, the Local Planning Authority (LPA) is responsible for monitoring the implementation of the permission. The LPA must ensure that it is carried out in accordance with the plans and any planning conditions.

To challenge a decision on a Crown Development application, an application must be made to the High Court. This must be made within 6 weeks of the day the decision is issued.
